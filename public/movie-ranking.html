The Best Movies According to Soumya

My top 3 Indian movies of all-time.

3 Idiots

Best for motivation + life lessons

🎓 1. It questions the education system The film shows how students are often pushed to memorize instead of understand. Through Aamir Khan’s character Rancho, it promotes: Learning with curiosity Creativity over rote learning Passion over pressure 😂 2. Perfect mix of comedy & emotion You’ll laugh a lot—but it also hits emotionally when it needs to. Iconic funny scenes (like “All is Well”) Deep emotional moments about friendship and family A balance very few movies achieve 🤝 3. Strong friendship story The bond between three friends feels real and relatable. Their journey shows: Supporting each other through struggles Finding your own path in life 💡 4. Life-changing message One of the biggest takeaways: “Follow excellence, success will chase you.” It encourages you to: Choose passion over societal expectations Think differently Not fear failure 🎬 5. Memorable characters & scenes Rancho (smart, free thinker) Virus (strict college director) Emotional backstories that stay with you.

Drishyam

Best for suspense & smart storytelling.

🧠 1. A story that outsmarts you The film follows a simple man, played by Ajay Devgn, who gets caught in a dangerous situation and uses intelligence—not power—to protect his family. What makes it special: Every detail matters Clues are planted early The story rewards attention 🔍 2. Edge-of-your-seat suspense The tension keeps building slowly. There are no unnecessary twists—everything is calculated and meaningful. Interrogation scenes feel real You’ll keep guessing what happens next The pressure on the family feels intense 🎭 3. Strong performances Especially from: Ajay Devgn (calm, intelligent, protective father) Tabu (powerful and relentless police officer) Their face-off scenes are some of the best parts of the movie. 🎯 4. One of the best endings ever Without spoiling it— the climax is clever, shocking, and deeply satisfying. It’s the kind of ending that makes you go: “Wow, that was smart.” 👨‍👩‍👧 5. Emotional core (family first) Beyond the thriller, it’s about: Protecting loved ones Moral dilemmas How far someone can go for family.

RRR

Best for action & cinematic experience.

If you’re looking for a movie that feels big, emotional, and unforgettable, RRR is exactly that. It’s not trying to be subtle—it’s designed to wow you at every moment. 💥 1. Insane action you won’t see anywhere else The action scenes are over-the-top in the best way—think hero vs wild animals, explosive fights, and gravity-defying stunts. It’s pure cinematic spectacle. 🤝 2. Powerful friendship story At its core, it’s about the bond between two men played by Ram Charan and Jr. NTR. Their chemistry makes the emotional moments hit hard—not just action, but real connection. 🎵 3. Music that became global The song “Naatu Naatu” isn’t just catchy—it won an Oscar and became a worldwide phenomenon. The dance sequence alone is worth watching. 🎥 4. Visuals + direction = next level Director S. S. Rajamouli is known for grand storytelling, and here he goes all out: Massive scale scenes Dramatic slow-motion shots Perfect mix of VFX and real emotion 🇮🇳 5. Emotional + patriotic vibe It’s loosely inspired by freedom fighters, so you get: Strong emotional highs Goosebumps moments A sense of pride and intensity.
